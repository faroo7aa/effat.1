Heritage Sites In Saudi Arabia

A stunning view of the scenic landscapes in the Jazan region, showcasing the natural beauty of Saudi Arabia.

The historic Najran Fort, a symbol of the rich history and culture of the Najran region.

The Edge of the World, a breathtaking natural landmark offering spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

Al-Faw Village, an archaeological site that reveals the ancient trade routes of the Arabian Peninsula.

Al-Baqi Cemetery, an important historical site located next to the Prophet's Mosque in Medina.

The ancient Sab'u Masajid, a group of historic mosques in the region, reflecting the early Islamic architecture.

Masjid al-Qiblatain, known for its unique significance in Islamic history as the mosque where the Qibla was changed.

Protecting heritage is essential for preserving the identity, culture, and traditions of a nation. Saudi Arabia takes pride in its historical sites and continues to invest in their protection, ensuring they are passed down to future generations.
